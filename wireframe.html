  1. 1App bar — "BearFamily" is your nucleus's name for the tree, not the tree's name: your sister's login says "my family", a cousin's says "FAM". Rename it any time. Search flies the canvas to any person; the "viewing as" chip is the profile picker for a shared nucleus login (so nicknames stay per person); −/+ zoom and "centre on me" live here too.
  2. 2Node = photo + your nickname + full name + flag. You call him "Atok", so that's his big label for you — a cousin sees "Pak Cik Mad" on the same node. The little flag pip top-right = where they live now (🇲🇾 🇦🇺 🇸🇬 at a glance across a scattered family). Initials fill in when there's no photo yet.
  3. 3Your node & nucleus — 3px primary ring + powder-blue halo + "you" chip; the halo sits outside the photo so a real photo can't swallow it. Your whole nucleus (Sarah, Alya, Aisha) is always in view — no focus or expansion ever parks it; only its own ⊖ can.
  4. 4⊕ expand pills — every edge person shows how many people are on their side (parents + siblings). Tap to grow the tree in that direction (frame 2).
  5. 5Collapsed relative chip — siblings not yet expanded stay as a quiet ghost pill so the default view stays calm.
  6. 6The canvas is a map, not a page — click & drag anywhere to pan any direction; the scroll wheel zooms at the cursor (Figma/Miro style), which is how a vertical-only mouse wheel stops fighting a sideways tree. shift-scroll pans sideways, trackpads pan both axes natively, pinch zooms.

  1. 1Your nickname — the one thing that's yours on someone else's profile. Edit inline; it instantly relabels their node on your tree.
  2. 2Also known as — read-only list of every nickname others have set for this person, grouped with who uses it. This is the "family mirror".
  3. 3Edit details / Add relative — anyone in the family can edit; changes sync for everyone (full names, photos, dates are shared; nicknames are per-viewer).
  4. 4Photo tile — tap to upload/crop; until then the initials placeholder shows on the tree.
  5. 5Relationship picker — a new person is always anchored to an existing one, so the tree can never have floating people. Launched from Granny's sheet, so she's pre-filled.
  6. 6Your own sheet flips the feature around: you see everything the family calls you. You can't edit these — they belong to the people who set them.
  7. 7Unverified until claimed — anyone can add a person who may never log in. While unclaimed, relatives within 5 branches (each partner or parent-child link = 1 branch) can edit the profile; beyond 5 it's view-only. Once someone claims it as themselves or their nucleus, only that nucleus can edit.
  8. 8Your nucleus card — who shares this login (one login per household, or per person — each family unit chooses), a reminder it's always in view, and your personal name for the tree.
  9. 9"Passed on" hides behind "+ more" (shown expanded here) — grief is never a face-up form field; the sheet shows only name, born and living-in until you ask for more. It's still the whole alive/dead model: one optional date field, empty means alive. Filling it greys the node, turns the photo greyscale and removes the flag pip (frame 2). The "Shown as" dropdown picks the memorial phrasing, grouped by tradition — Islam ("Returned to Allah on [date]", "Allahyarham/-ah"), Christian ("Went home to the Lord…"), Chinese ("安息 · at rest"), generic ("Passed on [date]") or custom — each with a with-date and a no-date form, so an unknown date simply reads "Passed" or "Allahyarhamah" (see Tok Mi vs Tok Wan in the tree frames). This phrasing stays editable by anyone within 5 branches even after the profile is claimed — the departed can't steward their own memorial line, so nearby family always can.
